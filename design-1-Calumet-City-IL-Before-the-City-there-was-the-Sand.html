Calumet City IL
Before the City there was the Sand

Location: Calumet City, IL
Size: 300-acre
Time: 2019
Client: Calumet City Concil
Award: Honor Award of American Society of Landscape Architects Award 2019
Category: Stormwater management / Geological /Landscape Urbanism
Funding: Illinois-Indiana Sea Grant Program (NOAA) 2018-2019 # NA18OAR4170082

This design project is part of a research initiative that investigates how to retrofit cities and neighborhoods through green infrastructure to address urban flooding. The research explores the hydro-geologic history of the Chicago-Calumet region and studies how native sub-soils that have been paved-over are an important part of better stormwater design across the area.

The site research specifically explored the geological origins of a 300-acre neighborhood in Calumet City, IL. The site and community are located within greater industrial Chicago and adjacent to one of the ecologically richest dune regions in the southern Great Lakes region. Working closely in partnership with Calumet City, the team of landscape architecture, geology, and civil & environmental engineering researchers and students combined soil fieldwork and mapping, stormwater modeling, and urban landscape design to address ongoing patterns of neighborhood flooding by designing new landscapes that reference and functionalize the hydrology and geology of the region.
