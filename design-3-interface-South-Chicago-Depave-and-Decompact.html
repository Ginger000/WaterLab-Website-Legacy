South Side Chicago : Depave & Decompact

Location: South Side Chicago, IL
Time: Spring semester, 2016
Course: Surface Design Studio - Depave Chicago
Affilitation: Landscape Architecture Department, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.
Category: Surface / Design / Soil /Landscape Urbanism

Interface addresses the condition of impervious surfaces and compacted soil that contribute to significant flooding in the South Side Chicago neighborhoods of Grand Boulevard and Washington Park. By identifying consecutive vacant city-owned properties, a test site was created to propose prototypical treatments to help mitigate compaction and flooding.

The project consists of three phases and enlists the help of cover crops, deep rooting perennials and organic matter to rebuild soil and to prepare the area for the planting of water-absorbing trees of oak, maple, beech, and hornbeam. Concrete and asphalt are cut to allow water flow, absorption, and re-direction into infiltration soils. Treatments for brick will allow a breaking of the surface and root penetration, creating a connection and exchange between the hydrosphere above and rhizosphere below the urban surface.
