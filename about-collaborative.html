WATER LAB Design ColLABorative


Stormwater needs a design revolution. Urban flooding, which is all too common across Chicago and southern Cook County, has been intensifying through climate change. We need a new approach to living with water and a citywide transition through ecological design. However, we believe this design revolution will be a community-based effort in collaboration with and supported by researchers, designers, and municipal leaders.

Water Lab's Design ColLABorative aims to facilitate that effort, by working directly with community organizations to think through the various problems associated with flooding - and by helping to apply design research to identify alternatives and possibilities.

Water Lab’s Depave Chicago project specifically targets the redesign of impervious and compacted surfaces around the city. With upwards of 30-50% of cities paved and compacted, we work to reverse and transform this condition in order to create more healthful living systems and surfaces in communities.
