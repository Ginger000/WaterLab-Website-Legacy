Paver Design @ Chicago River Edge

Location: South Side Chicago, IL
Time: Spring semester, 2016
Course: Surface Design Studio - Depave Chicago
Affilitation: Landscape Architecture Department, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.
Category: Surface / Design / Soil /Landscape Urbanism

The project “Just Add Water” envisions contemporary landscapes as platforms that celebrate nature and culture within the city. Within the selected site, flooding caused by vast impermeable surfaces is transformed from a problem into an aesthetic awareness for rain and water in Chicago. The design uses the idea of a thick 2D surface that increases perception of time, weather, and social spaces by capturing rain in visible patterns which also change use and movement across the site. The site was mapped according to its flooding pattern and as a historical-ecological transect from river edge to the neighborhood--from this, a modular paver design was created that emphasizes the ephemeral nature of environmental fluctuations of rainfall over seasons much like Braille writing for rain.
